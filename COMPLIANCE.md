# Compliance — PepTrack Growth OS

This system is built **compliance-first**. The rules below are load-bearing and enforced in the templates, the approval queue, and the data model.

## Non-negotiable guardrails (always on)

- **Draft-first, manual-send.** Nothing leaves the building without explicit approval. No auto-DM, no auto-email, no bots.
- **Public research only.** No scraping private groups/content. Contacts stay blank until a PUBLIC business contact is verified.
- **No medical claims.** PepTrack never treats, diagnoses, prevents, cures, or manages disease. It is a private tracking / organization / reminder / progress-journal tool.
- **No dosing advice, no protocols, no 'lose weight with this app', no 'replaces your doctor'.**
- **No brand drug names** (Ozempic/Wegovy/Mounjaro/Zepbound). Generic INN only, as a noun, never as advice.
- **Reviews:** Apple's native prompt only, after genuine positive-usage signals. Never incentivized; never money/discounts/perks; never deceptively route unhappy users to the store.
- **FTC:** every creator/affiliate promotion must carry clear `#ad` / paid-partnership disclosure; partners must acknowledge it (`creator_partners.disclosure_ack`).
- **CAN-SPAM / TCPA:** commercial email/SMS only to opted-in recipients, with sender identity + a working opt-out; cold outreach includes a respectful opt-out line.
- **Privacy:** PepTrack is local-first; the growth DB stores no end-user PII beyond anonymized review-eligibility refs.

## Risk register (from the automated audit)

> Audited the PepTrack outbound growth system across its real surfaces: the iOS native review prompt (Core/Services/ReviewPromptService.swift), the manual creator DM pack (instagram-launch-kit/wave1-dm-pack.md), the email/DM draft generator (scripts/outreach_drafts.py), the tiered creator CRM (instagram-launch-kit/creators.json), the per-creator affiliate landing template (marketing/creators/_template.html) with its App Store campaign token + clipboard hand-off, the 30-day community-posting calendar (incl. JORDAN10 cross-pollination), and the launch-engine playbook (~/.claude/skills/app-launch-growth-engine/SKILL.md).

The system is already strong on several non-negotiables: outreach is draft-first / manual-send (no auto-DM, no scraping), no review incentives anywhere, the iOS prompt uses the native AppStore.requestReview(in:) (throttled, once per version, after real engagement), creator screening excludes drug sellers/protocol-pushers, no brand drug names, and a "not a medical device" disclaimer is on every public surface. Secrets are gitignored and kept out of the Vercel-served dir, and SKILL.md codifies the hard lesson about not API-automating posts on new Meta accounts.

Material gaps to close (all concrete and fixable in templates/dashboard): (1) the generated cold *emails* in outreach_drafts.py carry NO unsubscribe mechanism and NO physical postal address — straight CAN-SPAM exposure; (2) NO #ad / "paid partnership" disclosure is generated or required anywhere, despite tier offers paying creators flat fees + performance bonuses (FTC + platform endorsement risk); (3) the per-creator landing pages are paid endorsements ("Recommended by __NAME__") with no affiliate/material-connection disclosure on the page; (4) cold email/DM drafts lack the respectful one-line opt-out the brief mandates; (5) the word "managing" in the positioning/POSITIONING string flirts with condition-management; (6) the clipboard hand-off (PT-<CODE>) reads the pasteboard on first launch and should be disclosed; (7) Reddit/TikTok/YouTube self-promo ToS aren't addressed the way Meta's are. Risk register below with the exact guardrail to bake in for each.

| Area | Risk | Issue | Guardrail enforced |
|---|---|---|---|
| can_spam | high | The email drafts generated by scripts/outreach_drafts.py (short_pitch, long_pitch, youtube_proposal, followup_1, followup_2, breakup) are commercial solicitation emails but contain NO unsubscribe mechanism and NO valid physical postal address. Both are required by the CAN-SPAM Act for commercial email regardless of B2B/creator context, with per-email penalties. A grep across scripts/ and instagram-launch-kit/ returns zero matches for unsubscribe/opt-out/physical address. | Bake a CAN-SPAM footer into every email template in outreach_drafts.py: (a) a one-line opt-out the recipient can act on by reply ('If you'd rather not hear from me, just reply and I'll remove you — no follow-up.'), (b) a valid physical postal address for the sender/PepTrack, (c) a truthful, non-deceptive Subject line (current subjects like 'I saved the CODE code for you' are borderline — keep them honest). Honor opt-outs within 10 business days: add an 'opted_out' status to creators.json so the CRM can never re-draft to that contact. DMs are not email but still get the opt-out line. |
| affiliate_disclosure | high | The per-creator landing pages (marketing/creators/_template.html) say 'Recommended by __NAME__' and '__NAME__ tracks with PepTrack' and hand out the creator's affiliate code — yet the tier offers (creators.json / outreach_drafts.py offer_for) pay creators a flat fee plus a performance bonus / affiliate payout on tracked installs. That is a paid material connection presented as an organic recommendation with NO disclosure on the page. FTC 16 CFR 255 requires clear, conspicuous disclosure of the paid/affiliate relationship at the point of the endorsement. | Add a visible, plain-language material-connection line to _template.html, near the headline/code (not buried in the footer): e.g. 'Paid partnership — __NAME__ earns a commission on installs from this page.' Make it non-removable in the generator. Require every creator contract/brief to mandate #ad or 'paid partnership' on the creator's own posts/Reels/videos/Stories and in the YouTube description, and add a CRM field (e.g. disclosure_confirmed) that must be true before a code is activated. |
| ftc_endorsement | high | The outreach offers (outreach_drafts.py: Founding Creator Partner = flat fee + performance bonus; Sponsored Integration; affiliate payout on tracked installs) create endorser relationships, but no generated draft, asset kit reference, or CRM gate requires the creator to disclose the paid/affiliate nature in their content. The drafts say 'co-created compliant content' and 'I supply the assets' without ever instructing the creator to label it as an ad. Under the FTC 2023 endorsement guides, the brand is liable for undisclosed paid endorsements and for any health/result claims a paid creator makes. | Add a mandatory 'Disclosure & claims rules' block to every outreach pack and the creator kit: (1) clear #ad / 'paid partnership' on all sponsored content and the disclosure must be hard to miss (not buried in hashtags); (2) the creator may NOT make medical/result/weight-loss claims or show before/after-as-proof tied to the app; (3) the brand reviews content for compliance before it goes live. Track in the CRM: disclosure_confirmed + content_reviewed flags gate payout. Never pay on, or keep live, an integration that lacks visible disclosure. |
| medical_claim | medium | The core positioning string in outreach_drafts.py (POSITIONING) and several drafts/DMs describe PepTrack as 'for people managing GLP-1s and peptides' and 'a clean, private home for the whole protocol.' 'Managing' and 'protocol' edge toward condition/treatment-management framing the brief prohibits. Separately, paid creators (a controlled channel) are the highest-risk place for a stray 'lose weight with this app' or dosing comment to appear and be attributable to PepTrack. | Replace 'managing GLP-1s and peptides' with 'tracking GLP-1s and peptides' across POSITIONING and all templates; keep 'protocol' only as the user's own private record, never as advice. Hard-rule in the creator kit and dashboard linter: no treat/diagnose/prevent/cure/manage-a-condition language, no dosing/protocol guidance, no 'lose weight with this app', no brand drug names (semaglutide/tirzepatide as a search noun only). Keep the existing 'educational and personal tracking only — not a medical device' line on every public/creator surface (already present — preserve it). |
| apple_review | low | The iOS review flow (ReviewPromptService.swift) is well-built: native AppStore.requestReview(in:), fired only after >=4 logged events, at most once per app version, with Apple's own ~3x/year cap — no custom rating UI, no incentive, no nag. The only residual exposure is system-wide consistency: the affiliate code / founding perks must never be gated on or tied to leaving a review (App Store 5.6 + 1MDC), and marketing must not direct users to rate the app in exchange for anything. | Keep using ONLY the native StoreKit prompt — never a custom 'rate us' modal, never deep-link to the write-review URL as a reward step. Add an explicit rule to the dashboard/templates: no code, discount, unlock, giveaway entry, or creator perk may be conditioned on writing a review or rating, and no outreach/post may ask for reviews in exchange for anything. Leave the throttle/threshold logic as-is. |
| platform_policy | medium | SKILL.md thoroughly addresses Meta/Instagram automation (the hard lesson: Meta blocked Hart Vintage's API for day-one automated posting on a new account; default to manual/native for 2-4 weeks). But the system spans YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit-style community posting without equivalent codified ToS guardrails. Reddit in particular treats unlabeled founder self-promotion and code-dropping as spam/ban-worthy; TikTok/YouTube require ad disclosure via their built-in paid-promotion toggles, not just on-screen #ad. | Add per-platform self-promo rules to the dashboard: (1) Meta — keep the existing 2-4-week manual/native warmup + Business Verification before any API posting, low-volume thereafter, never auto-DM (already codified). (2) Reddit — only participate where self-promo is allowed, follow each subreddit's promo ratio/rules, disclose founder status, never spam codes. (3) TikTok/YouTube — require creators to enable the platform's paid-promotion / 'includes paid promotion' toggle IN ADDITION to spoken/written #ad. (4) No bots, auto-follow, bought followers, or engagement pods on any platform. |
| privacy | medium | Two items. (a) The creator CRM (creators.json) stores real people's contact_email and handles; even though only public business contacts are collected, maintaining that contact database makes PepTrack a data controller with notice/retention/opt-out duties, and the file must never become public. (b) The per-creator landing page (_template.html) writes 'PT-<CODE>' to the visitor's clipboard on App Store tap and the app reads the pasteboard on first launch — iOS shows a paste banner, and silent clipboard reads are a known privacy sensitivity. | For the CRM: collect public business contacts only (already a rule), keep creators.json out of any public/Vercel-served path, honor reply-to-remove + 'opted_out' status, and don't retain contacts of those who declined beyond a suppression flag. For the clipboard hand-off: the page already shows 'Code saved — opening the App Store…'; keep that transparency, scope the in-app read to a one-time first-launch attribution check (as designed), and never read the clipboard silently/repeatedly. Disclose the attribution-code behavior in the privacy policy. |
| data_storage | low | The architecture is sound (app is local-first/iCloud, secrets gitignored and kept out of the Vercel-served marketing/ dir per SKILL.md). The remaining storage risk is operational: creators.json now holds, or will hold, real creator emails and partnership/payout notes, and outreach-drafts/<id>.md packs embed those contacts — if the iOS repo (getplnr/peptrack-ios) is or becomes public, that PII and the partner pipeline leak. | Gitignore the populated CRM and generated packs (creators.json with real rows, outreach-drafts/*.md) or keep the repo private; ship only the placeholder/sample row in any public template. Run 'git check-ignore' on tokens before every commit (already the rule), and never paste service-role keys / app secrets / Resend keys into chat or into marketing/. Apply data-minimization: store only what's needed for outreach + attribution, not extraneous personal data. |
